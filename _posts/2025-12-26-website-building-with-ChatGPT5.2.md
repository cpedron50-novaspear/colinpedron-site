I built out this website using AI-generated HTML code inserted into GitHub, and I hosted it on Cloudflare. No frameworks, templates, or paid web-building tools needed. 

Only real cost (other than my time) was the $10 annual fee that I paid to Cloudflare to purchase this domain name (colinpedron.com). That'll renew automatically each year for the rest of time, or at least until my credit card stops working. 

I used ChatGPT 5.2 for all aspects of setup for this site. The main design constraint was ease-of-maintenance. I wanted something I could update quickly without creating future work.

There were a few hiccups along the way (such as the fact that the original link needed to be HTTPS for web safety, which is harder to set up than you'd thing). All issues were ultimately resolved over the course of 2 days. I took lots of little pauses along the way to ask the AI model to explain further. Typically this consisted of me sending screenshots of error messages or (more commonly) asking it to rewind back to earlier instructions to remove coder jargon and just explain what I needed to do in plain english. 

This type of website setup is predictable, cheap to run, and perhaps a little boring for those with more coding experience. As somebody with just a little coding experience but a whole lot of AI experience, I'll take the simplistic back-end design any day, because I know that the AI model can guide me through any wierd issues that might crop up while still making the actual site itself appear professional. That being said, the simplicity itself prevents many issues from emerging in the first place, which is nice.
